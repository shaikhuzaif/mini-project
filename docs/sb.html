Swachh Bharat

What is Swachh Bharat?

Swachh Bharat, also known as the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan or Clean India Mission, is a nationwide cleanliness and sanitation campaign launched by the Government of India in 2014. The initiative aims to achieve the vision of a clean and open defecation-free India by October 2, 2019, marking the 150th birth anniversary of Mahatma Gandhi, the Father of the Nation.

Swachh Bharat focuses on various aspects of cleanliness and sanitation, including the construction of toilets, proper waste management, behavior change, and awareness generation. The campaign addresses both rural and urban areas, targeting different sections of society, with a particular emphasis on improving access to sanitation facilities for marginalized communities.

Swachh Bharat, meaning "Clean India," is a national cleanliness and sanitation campaign launched by the Government of India. The initiative focuses on promoting cleanliness, hygiene, and proper sanitation practices across the country.

Sustainable Development Goals: Swachh Bharat aligns with the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly Goal 6, which emphasizes access to clean water and sanitation for all. Achieving the goals of Swachh Bharat contributes to broader sustainable development objectives, including health, education, gender equality, and poverty alleviation.
